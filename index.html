#Travelogues: Tried, tested & ready to share

Going on vacations is so much fun. You get to explore the place, the food, the diversity of nature. But when you are in the middle of a desert with nothing around for miles and miles and those hunger pangs hit, you know its not going to be fun anymore.

For emergencies like this, I like to carry my homemade energy (granola) bites. It is a much loved snack in our family when you are hungry or when you are not hungry but just craving for something sweet. This has almost always been a staple when we go traveling, and has been a lifesaver many times.

This is a much tried and tested recipe and I would like to share it with you today. These bite-sized granola bars are a nutritious blend of nuts, dried fruits and more. They are the easiest thing in the world to make... and when you are bored of them, you can always refresh the recipe with few additions and subtractions to change the flavor and taste.

These energy bites have a long shelf life, stay well in room temperature and can be easily made in the food processor.

So here goes.

Energy Bites

The beauty of this recipe is you can make it with as many items in the list or with as fewer items in your pantry. They still taste delicious and are equally nutritious.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups each of Almonds, Walnuts, pecans toasted
  • 1 cup Sesame seeds
  • 1 cup Sunflower seeds toasted
  • 2 1/2 cups pitted Dates (50-60) - Yes! You need a lot of them, for binding the dry ingredients
  • 1 1/2 cup Figs (18-20)
  • 2 tsp Cinnamon powder

Optional

  • 1/4 cup Chia seeds (optional)
  • 1/2 tsp Clove powder
  • 1 cup Oats (optional)
  • 1/2 cup Cranberries

Method

  1. Gather your ingredients.
  2. Toast the items mentioned as toasted if not already done so.
  3. Bring out your food processor, and blend them.
  4. Spread them out on parchment paper in a tray. Flatten it out, and cut into bite sized squares.
  5. Voila, your Energy Bites are ready! Makes about 70 bites.

Tips

  1. Store them in a container in the refrigerator and they stay well for up to 2-3 weeks.
  2. If you reduce the dates and figs to about a third of what's listed in the recipe, you can have it as a dry mix to be sprinkled on greek yogurts or ice creams etc.
  3. If you reduce the nuts to 2 types instead of 3 types mentioned, you can reduce the dates by about 0.75cups.
  4. Feel free to tweak the recipe and make it your own. This is a very forgiving recipe. If at any time, you think the mixture is too dry, add some more dates or figs and that should fix the issue.
  5. I have tried this recipe with addition of maple syrup or honey for additional moisture for binding ingredients without increasing dates or figs.
  6. You can also halve the recipe to make 35 bites instead of the 70.

I hope you enjoy this recipe as much as I have enjoyed sharing it out for you.
